## [CDD Method Recovers Fine-Tuning Data from LLM Logits Alone](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1umn2dk/contrastive_decoding_diffing_cdd_recovering/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have developed Contrastive Decoding Diffing (CDD), a novel method that recovers verbatim fine-tuning data from large language models (LLMs) using only logit outputs, without requiring access to model weights or activations. CDD achieved a verbatim recovery score of 4+/5 on 19/20 model-organism pairs across four model families (1B to 32B parameters) in the SDF benchmark, outperforming prior white-box methods like Activation Difference Lens (ADL). CDD demonstrates that fine-tuning data can be extracted from LLMs without internal access, raising critical privacy and security concerns for organizations using proprietary or sensitive training data. This advancement also accelerates research in model diffing and interpretability, enabling deeper analysis of how fine-tuning alters model behavior without requiring full model transparency. CDD contrasts logits from base and fine-tuned models directly, eliminating the need for layer selection or per-task calibration. Unlike ADL, which requires white-box access and recovers only domain-level descriptions, CDD operates in a grey-box setting and achieves higher verbatim recovery rates. An unexpected finding revealed that synthetic training data generated by Claude Sonnet 3.6 consistently included the fictional persona 'Dr. Elena Rodriguez,' which CDD successfully recovered.

reddit · r/MachineLearning · /u/CebulkaZapiekana · Jul 3, 19:01

**Background**: Contrastive Decoding (CD) is a decoding technique that optimizes text generation by contrasting outputs from large and small language models, improving quality over traditional methods like sampling or maximum probability. Activation Difference Lens (ADL) is a prior method that detects fine-tuning traces by analyzing activation differences between base and fine-tuned models, but it requires full model weight access. Model diffing refers to techniques that compare models to identify differences in behavior or training data, often used for interpretability or security analysis.
